General

General Members

General Members are practicing psychiatrists who have completed an acceptable residency training program in psychiatry, and hold either a valid license to practice medicine or an academic, research, or governmental position that does not require licensure.

Early Career Psychiatrists (ECPs) are General Members of the APA who are within their first seven years after completion of training (ACGME accredited residency/fellowship). Dues are reduced for the first six years of General Membership to ease the financial burdens of early career psychiatrists.

Benefits and Services

Publications

  • Journals
    • Receive articles on cutting-edge research and the latest developments in your field with free subscriptions to American Journal of Psychiatry and Psychiatric News ($318 value)
    • Complimentary online subscription to Focus: The Journal of Lifelong Learning in Psychiatry for ECPs ($569 value)
    • Discounted subscription to Psychiatric Services, APA's monthly journal focusing on the delivery of behavioral health services in organized systems ($40 value)

  • Subscriptions to online newsletters: Psychiatric News Update, APA Advocacy Update, Integrated Care Newsletter, and Psychiatric Practice and Managed Care

e-Learning

  • Free online CME learning modules to advance clinical and professional competencies including self-assessment and performance in practice modules (over $500 value)
  • Try the APA Learning Center free with the Members’ Course of the Month. Each month members have free access to an on-demand CME course on a popular topic.

Live Learning

  • Registration discounts to APA Annual Meeting ($600 value) and IPS: The Mental Health Services Conference ($190 value)

Practice Tools

  • Indispensible practice management tools including Practice Management Help Line, Coding Services, and Practice Guidelines

  • Inclusion in Find a Psychiatrist database that helps patients find you

  • APA-endorsed members only malpractice insurance program administered by American Professional Agency, Inc.

Added Benefits

District Branch and State Association Dues

The APA and the District Branches/State Associations have a dual membership requirement. Members must join both organizations. Members in California, Connecticut, and New York belong to the state association and local district branch. The state-level dues help sponsor professional development opportunities, local meetings for networking and continuing educational programs, newsletters , and even state-level lobbying efforts for the advancement of psychiatry.

APA assists some District Branches in collecting dues but is not liable for any decisions or actions of the District Branches or State Associations. District Branch/State Association dues range from $60-$600.

Once you submit your membership application you will be sent an invoice with exact amount of membership dues owed.
